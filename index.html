Ranking Top 5 Fortnite Emotes

The hit game Fortnite has featured over 1000 emotes since its release on July 21, 2017, but today, some stand out more than the others.

5. Electro Swing

A staple for players with appreciation for vintage flair, the "Electro Swing" emote brings a big band jazz beat with a modern house twist. Introduced in Season 6, this emote's smooth, snappy moves and catchy music make it a popular choice for those who want to add some classy style to their victory celebrations.

4. Billy Bounce

Known as a top "toxic" emote by the community, "Billy Bounce" is a quick dance that is often used to flex on opponents after a clutch elimination. Its frequent use and memorable music make it a popular choice for celebrations.

3. Orange Justice

This quirky dance move started with a contest entry by a young player whose dance submission was passed over for the top prize. Epic Games later added it to the Season 4 Battle Pass due to overwhelming fan demand, immortalizing the dance in Fortnite history.

2. Floss

Made popular by the "Backpack Kid," the "Floss" was introduced in the Season 2 Battle Pass in 2018. It is no longer available, with its notoriety and scarcity making it a celebrated emote among original players.

1. Take the L

One of the most famous and toxic emotes in the game, "Take the L" is a classic way to mock an eliminated opponent. The emote was featured in the Season 3 Battle Pass in 2018 and has not been available since, making it a status symbol for older players.
